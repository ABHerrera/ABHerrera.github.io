"Never regret a day in your life: good days give happiness, bad days give experience, worst days give lessons, and best days give memories." ~ Unknown


Simple ways to find bliss in your life:

  • Write down the moments that you feel happy
  • Set aside time each night to reflect on the day
  • Take time to slow down and experience each moment
  • Say 'no thanks' to the things that make you unhappy
  • Spend more time doing the things you love
  • Spend more time with the people that make you happy
  • Smile just because
  • Spend more time in nature
  • Eat healthy unprocessed food (it'll make you feel good from the inside out)

The Science of Happiness

The idea of gratitude is all the rage in women's and men's magazines, in health publications, and in international publications like the The New York Times. Think all this "happiness stuff" is just some hippy dippie trend? It is a trend. A trend that has lasted thousands of years and originially started by Buddhist monks. The idea of actively focusing on activities that increase happiness just recently came into mainstream Western culture. Largely because science has now proved what the Buddhists have known for thousands of years...training your mind to be happy is like training to complete a marathon. If you have never run a marathon in your life when you start running you may run a bit but you get tired so you have to keep practicing to keep gain endurance. Same goes for our happiness. Sure we experience daily moments of happiness but we get distracted by things that happen everyday life...maybe someone you love is in a bad mood, maybe you get cut off on the freeway, etc. So in order to have our daily happiness last longer we have to actively engage in activities that will increase our "happiness endurance".

If you prefer a scientific explanation for why you should take a few moments each day to increase your happiness check out these resources: Habits of Happiness and The Habits of Happiness and Buddha's Brain 'Neuroplasticity and Meditation'.
